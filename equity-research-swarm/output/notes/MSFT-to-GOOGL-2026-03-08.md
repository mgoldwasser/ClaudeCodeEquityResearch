## Observation

Microsoft announced M365 price increases effective July 2026 (E3: $36->$39, E5: $57->$60) that will include bundled Copilot AI features in base M365 subscriptions. This directly mirrors Google's January 2025 strategy of bundling Gemini free in Google Workspace. Simultaneously, Microsoft's Copilot seat growth (+160% YoY to 15M paid seats) and Azure's AI engagement advantage (45% vs. 25% cloud share) suggest Microsoft is winning the enterprise AI adoption race despite Google's pricing advantage. [Source: Microsoft IR Q2 FY2026, Directions on Microsoft, Revolgy 2026]

## Implication for Target

Google's "free Gemini in Workspace" strategy appears to be gaining consumer traction (750M MAU, 18-21% AI chatbot web traffic share) but has not yet translated into enterprise M365 displacement. Microsoft's decision to bundle Copilot features into base M365 pricing (via the July 2026 increase) neutralizes Google's pricing advantage while raising ARPU. If Microsoft successfully converts even 30% of its 446M M365 seats to AI-enabled tiers, Google Workspace's enterprise AI value proposition weakens. Google Cloud analysts should assess whether Google's 34% cloud growth and Gemini bundling strategy can be sustained if Microsoft matches on bundling.

## Supporting Evidence

- Google bundled Gemini in Workspace at no additional cost (January 2025) [Tier 1]
- M365 Copilot: 15M paid seats (+160% YoY) vs. Gemini: 27M enterprise users [Tier 1]
