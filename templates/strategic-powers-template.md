## Purpose

This template connects competitive strategy to financial modeling. Each Power maps to a specific financial variable that the DCF Analyst uses. The goal is not to score moats abstractly — it is to determine which financial assumptions are *justified* by the company's strategic position and which are *fragile*.

**Analytical chain:** Market dynamics (TAM/demand) → Strategic positioning (this template) → Cash flow assumptions (DCF inputs)

---

## 1. Power-by-Power Assessment

For each of the 7 Powers, assess: (a) whether the company possesses it, (b) the evidence, (c) the financial variable it protects, (d) the decay timeline.

### Power 2: Network Effects

**Definition:** The value of the product/service increases as more users adopt it, creating a self-reinforcing competitive advantage.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Present?** | [Yes / Partial / No] |
| **Type** | [Direct (same-side: more users → more value for users) / Indirect (cross-side: more users → more developers/content → more value) / Data (more users → better product via data)] |
| **Evidence** | [e.g., User growth rate accelerating despite no increase in marketing spend; developer ecosystem growing X% YoY; data advantage measurable in model performance vs. competitors] |
| **Magnitude** | [Strength: marginal user adds $X value to existing users; or: switching to competitor loses access to N connections/integrations] |
| **Financial variable protected** | **Customer acquisition cost (CAC) and retention** — Network effects reduce CAC over time and increase retention |
| **DCF implication** | [e.g., "Supports declining S&M as % of revenue from 25% to 18%" or "Supports >95% net revenue retention"] |
| **Decay risk** | [e.g., Multi-homing (users on multiple platforms simultaneously); network forking; open-source alternatives replicating the network] |
| **Decay timeline** | [e.g., "Strong for 3-5 years; at risk if open-source alternative reaches critical mass"] |
| **Score (1-10)** | [Score] |

### Power 3: Counter-Positioning

**Definition:** A newcomer adopts a superior business model that the incumbent cannot copy without damaging their existing business. The incumbent's rational response is to *not* respond.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Present?** | [Yes / Partial / No — and specify: is the subject company the counter-positioner or the incumbent being counter-positioned?] |
| **Evidence** | [e.g., Subject company's model (subscription) vs. incumbent's model (perpetual license); incumbent would cannibalize $XB in existing revenue to match; incumbent has attempted half-measures that failed] |
| **Magnitude** | [Incumbent's cost of response: cannibalization of $X revenue; organizational restructuring; channel conflict] |
| **Financial variable protected** | **Competitive response timeline** — Counter-positioning buys time before incumbents can effectively respond |
| **DCF implication** | [e.g., "Supports 3-5 years of above-market growth before competitive response" or "Not applicable — subject IS the incumbent being counter-positioned, which is a risk"] |
| **Decay risk** | [e.g., Incumbent finds a way to offer both models; new entrant leapfrogs both] |
| **Decay timeline** | [e.g., "Incumbent response likely in 2-4 years based on similar transitions in adjacent markets"] |
| **Score (1-10)** | [Score] |

### Power 4: Switching Costs

**Definition:** The cost (financial, procedural, relational) a customer incurs to switch to a competitor's product.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Present?** | [Yes / Partial / No] |
| **Type** | [Financial (contractual penalties, migration costs) / Procedural (retraining, workflow disruption, data migration) / Relational (loss of customization, relationship, institutional knowledge)] |
| **Evidence** | [e.g., Average migration cost $XM per enterprise customer; 18-month typical migration timeline; churn rate X% vs. industry average Y%; customer survey data on switching friction] |
| **Magnitude** | [Quantified: switching cost = $X per customer, or X months of revenue, or X% of customer's annual IT budget] |
| **Financial variable protected** | **Revenue retention / churn rate** — High switching costs justify low churn and high net revenue retention assumptions |
| **DCF implication** | [e.g., "Supports 95%+ gross retention rate and 110%+ net retention" or "Switching costs are moderate — churn assumption of X% may be optimistic"] |
| **Decay risk** | [e.g., API standardization reducing migration friction; cloud-native competitors reducing procedural switching costs; regulatory mandates requiring data portability] |
| **Decay timeline** | [e.g., "API standardization 2-3 years away; data portability regulation 3-5 years"] |
| **Score (1-10)** | [Score] |

### Power 5: Branding

**Definition:** A durable attribution of higher value to an objectively similar product, allowing price premiums or lower customer acquisition costs.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Present?** | [Yes / Partial / No] |
| **Evidence** | [e.g., Price premium of X% over comparable competitor products; brand awareness X% in target market; NPS score X vs. industry average Y; brand mentioned unprompted in X% of enterprise RFPs] |
| **Magnitude** | [Price premium: X% above commodity alternative; or: CAC X% lower than unbranded competitor] |
| **Financial variable protected** | **Pricing premium sustainability** — Branding justifies ASP assumptions above commodity levels |
| **DCF implication** | [e.g., "Supports ASP growth of X% per year vs. industry flat/declining" or "Brand is functional (trust/reliability), not aspirational — supports stability, not premium growth"] |
| **Decay risk** | [e.g., Brand damage from product failure; generational shift in buyer preferences; commoditization of category making brand less relevant] |
| **Decay timeline** | [e.g., "Enterprise brands are sticky — 5-10 year decay; consumer brands can shift faster"] |
| **Score (1-10)** | [Score] |

### Power 6: Cornered Resource

**Definition:** Preferential access to a coveted asset that independently enhances value — talent, IP, regulatory license, natural resource, data.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Present?** | [Yes / Partial / No] |
| **Resource type** | [Talent / IP (patents, trade secrets) / Regulatory license / Natural resource / Proprietary data / Supply agreement] |
| **Evidence** | [e.g., Patent portfolio of X patents with Y years remaining; exclusive supply agreement with Z through 2030; proprietary dataset of X records not replicable; regulatory license with X-year renewal cycle; key talent retention rate X%] |
| **Magnitude** | [e.g., Patent protection covers X% of revenue; supply agreement provides X% cost advantage; data advantage produces X% better model performance] |
| **Financial variable protected** | **Supply constraint duration / competitive entry barrier** — Cornered resources justify sustained competitive advantage assumptions |
| **DCF implication** | [e.g., "Supports margin premium for X years until patent expiration" or "Data advantage supports X% better unit economics vs. new entrants for forecast period"] |
| **Decay risk** | [e.g., Patent expiration timeline; talent poaching; data becoming commoditized; regulatory changes opening access; supply agreement renegotiation] |
| **Decay timeline** | [e.g., "Key patents expire 2029-2031; data advantage has 3-5 year half-life as competitors accumulate"] |
| **Score (1-10)** | [Score] |

### Power 7: Process Power

**Definition:** Embedded organizational capabilities enabling lower costs or superior products that competitors cannot replicate because the advantage resides in complex, evolved processes rather than discrete, transferable assets.

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Present?** | [Yes / Partial / No] |
| **Evidence** | [e.g., Manufacturing yield X% vs. industry average Y%; R&D-to-revenue cycle X months vs. competitor Y months; defect rate X ppm vs. industry Z ppm; operational metric X consistently outperforms peers across cycles] |
| **Magnitude** | [e.g., Process advantage generates X% lower COGS or X% higher output per employee; advantage has persisted through N management transitions and M economic cycles] |
| **Financial variable protected** | **Margin advantage sustainability** — Process power justifies persistent margin premium over competitors |
| **DCF implication** | [e.g., "Supports gross margin premium of X bps over sector median throughout forecast period" or "Process advantages are visible but likely replicable within 2-3 years — do not assume persistent premium"] |
| **Decay risk** | [e.g., Key personnel departure; technology paradigm shift making current processes obsolete; competitor reverse-engineering through poaching or benchmarking] |
| **Decay timeline** | [e.g., "Process power is the most durable advantage — typically 5-10 year half-life if organizationally embedded"] |
| **Score (1-10)** | [Score] |
