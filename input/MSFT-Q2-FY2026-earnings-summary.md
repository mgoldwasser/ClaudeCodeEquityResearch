# Microsoft Q2 FY2026 Earnings Summary
Quarter Ended: December 31, 2025
Report Date: January 28, 2026
Source: Microsoft IR Press Release + Motley Fool Transcript

## Financial Highlights
- **Total Revenue:** $81.3B (+17% YoY, +15% CC)
- **Operating Income:** $38.3B (+21% YoY, +19% CC)
- **Net Income (GAAP):** $38.5B (+60% YoY) -- includes $7.6B net gains from OpenAI investments
- **Net Income (non-GAAP):** $30.9B (+23% YoY, +21% CC)
- **Diluted EPS (GAAP):** $5.16 (+60% YoY)
- **Diluted EPS (non-GAAP):** $4.14 (+24% YoY, +21% CC)

## Segment Results
| Segment | Revenue | Growth | Growth (CC) |
|---------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Productivity & Business Processes | $34.1B | +16% | +14% |
| Intelligent Cloud | $32.9B | +29% | +28% |
| More Personal Computing | $14.3B | -3% | -3% |

## Key Metrics
- **Microsoft Cloud Revenue:** $51.5B (+26% YoY, +24% CC) -- first time above $50B
- **Azure & Cloud Services Growth:** +39% YoY (+38% CC)
- **Commercial RPO:** $625B (+110% YoY); ~45% from OpenAI (~$280B)
- **Capital Expenditures (H1 FY26):** $49.3B (Q1: $34.9B, Q2 est ~$37.5B)
- **Free Cash Flow (H1 FY26):** $31.5B
- **Shareholder Returns (Q2):** $12.7B in dividends and buybacks (+32% YoY)
- **GitHub Copilot:** 4.7M paid subscribers (+75% YoY)
- **Microsoft 365 Copilot:** 15M paid seats, 160% YoY seat growth
- **Windows 11:** 1B users (+45%)
- **Fabric:** $2B+ ARR, +60% YoY growth, 28,000 paying customers

## Q3 FY2026 Guidance
- **Revenue:** $80.65B - $81.75B
- **Azure Growth:** ~37-38% (CC)
- **Cloud Gross Margin:** ~65% (down YoY)
- **CapEx:** Expected to decrease sequentially
- **FY2026 Operating Margins:** Up slightly YoY

## CEO Satya Nadella Key Quotes
- On GPU allocation: "If I had taken the GPUs that just came online in Q1 and Q2...and allocated them all to Azure, the KPI would have been over 40."
- On AI strategy: Three stack layers -- cloud/token factory, agent platform, agentic experiences
- "Tokens per watt per dollar" as optimization metric
- "50% increase in throughput" on OpenAI inferencing
- Maya 200 accelerator: "over 30% improved TCO"
- "Broadest selection of models of any hyperscaler" -- offering GPT-5.0.2, Claude 4.5

## CFO Amy Hood Key Points
- GPU allocation priority: (1) first-party Copilot apps, (2) R&D, (3) Azure capacity
- "Demand continues to exceed available supply"
- CapEx funds multiple AI products beyond Azure alone

## Notable Items
- Gaming revenue declined 9% in constant currency
- Memory pricing could create volatility in PC and server markets
- $7.6B one-time gain from OpenAI October recapitalization (non-GAAP excluded)
