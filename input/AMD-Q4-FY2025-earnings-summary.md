- Embedded: $950M (+2.9% YoY)

## Segment Revenue (FY2025)
- Data Center: $16,635M (+32% YoY)
- Client: $10,640M
- Gaming: $3,910M
- Embedded: $3,454M

## Q1 2026 Guidance
- Revenue: ~$9.8B (+/- $300M)
- Includes ~$100M MI308 China sales
- YoY growth: ~32%
- Sequential decline: ~5% (seasonality)
- Non-GAAP gross margin: ~55%

## Key Management Commentary (Lisa Su)
- "Data center segment revenue [to grow] by more than 60% annually, over the next three to five years"
- "Scale our AI business to tens of billions in annual revenue in 2027"
- Revenue CAGR target: >35% over 3-5 years
- Annual EPS target: >$20 in strategic timeframe
- OpenAI relationship "on track" for H2 MI450 ramp
- "Customer pull for Venice [next-gen EPYC] is very high"
- China remains "a very dynamic situation" — no forecast beyond Q1
- Semi-custom gaming to decline significantly due to console cycle maturity; next-gen Xbox expected 2027

## CFO (Jean Hu) Commentary
- Q4 gross margin benefited from $306M inventory reserve release
- Record free cash flow of $2.1B in Q4 (FY2025 FCF ~$6.7B)
- Projected continued margin improvement as MI450 scales
- Share repurchase program active

## Notable Items
- $800M inventory charge in 2025 related to MI308/China export controls
- Total 2025 revenue impact from export controls: $1.5-1.8B
- EPYC Turin >50% of server revenue in Q4
- ~1,600 EPYC cloud instances (+50% YoY)
- MI350 deployed at 8 of 10 top AI companies
- 500+ AMD-based cloud instances launched in 2025
