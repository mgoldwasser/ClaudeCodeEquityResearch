## Q4 2025 Earnings Call (January 28, 2026)

### Mark Zuckerberg — CEO

**On AI infrastructure investment:**
"I expect our first models will be good, but more importantly, we'll show the rapid trajectory that we're on."

"I expect us to steadily push the frontier over the course of the year, as we continue to release new models."

**On content and AI-generated feeds:**
"Soon, we'll see an explosion of new media formats that are more immersive and interactive, and only possible because of advances in AI."

**On AI monetization:**
Zuckerberg indicated there will be opportunities for "subscriptions and advertising" with Meta AI, confirming plans for a premium AI subscription model.

**On business performance:**
"We had strong business performance in 2025. I'm looking forward to advancing personal superintelligence for people around the world in 2026."

**On AI infrastructure (AI-as-infrastructure framing):**
Meta framed 2026 capex as "front-loading" infrastructure capacity to prepare for "the most optimistic cases" in AI development.

**On Vision for AI:**
Meta's new TBD AI research unit (established after Llama 4 underperformed expectations) is testing a frontier model code-named "Avocado" (Llama successor), targeting release H1 2026.

### Susan Li — CFO

**On Q1 2026 guidance:**
- Revenue guidance: $53.5B–$56.5B (vs. $51.3B Wall Street estimate at time of call)
- ~4% FX tailwind embedded in guidance

**On 2026 full-year expenses:**
- Total expenses: $162B–$169B
- Capital expenditures: $115B–$135B (includes finance lease principal)
- Tax rate: 13%–16%

**On operating income commitment:**
"Despite the meaningful step up in infrastructure investment, in 2026 we expect to deliver operating income that is above 2025 operating income."

**On Reality Labs:**
"We expect that Q4 Reality Labs revenue will be lower than last year for a couple reasons." (Q3 2025 call)
Reality Labs 2026 operating losses expected to remain at 2025 levels.

**On legal/litigation:**
CFO stated that trials scheduled for 2026 "may ultimately result in a material loss" (youth mental health lawsuits).

## Q3 2025 Earnings Call (October 29, 2025)
**Source:** Motley Fool transcript

### Mark Zuckerberg — CEO

**On AI infrastructure:**
"I think that it's the right strategy to aggressively front-load building capacity so that way we're prepared for the most optimistic cases."

"We're seeing very high demand for additional compute both internally and externally."

**On user engagement:**
"We had another strong quarter with 3.5 billion people using at least one of our apps every day."

"Video time spent on Instagram up more than 30% since last year."

**On Ray-Ban Meta glasses:**
"The response so far has been great. The new Ray-Ban Meta glasses and Oakley Meta Vanguards are both selling well."

**On Meta AI:**
Meta AI reached 1 billion monthly active users globally (reported May 2025), with ~40M daily active users and 185M weekly active users.

**On Advantage+ monetization:**
"The annual run rate of revenue running through our end-to-end automated solutions has now reached $60 billion." (as of Q3 2025)

### Susan Li — CFO

**On Q3 ad revenue:**
"Ad revenue was $50.1 billion, up 26% or 25% on a constant currency basis."

**On 2026 expense outlook:**
"Total expenses will grow at a significantly faster percentage rate in 2026 than 2025."
"CapEx dollar growth will be notably larger in 2026 than 2025."

## Key Management Themes (Synthesized Across Calls)

1. **AI-First Transformation:** Company pivoting from social media company to AI infrastructure + AI services platform. "Personal Superintelligence" is the north star.

2. **Front-Loading CapEx:** Management explicitly defends massive 2026 CapEx increase as necessary to capture AI opportunity. Does not provide quantified ROI timeline.

3. **Advertising AI Virtuous Cycle:** AI improvements in ad targeting, ranking, and creative generation driving measurable ad revenue outperformance. Advantage+ automated solutions at $60B run rate.

4. **Reality Labs Strategic Pivot:** Formal abandonment of metaverse/VR as primary RL focus. Pivot to AI glasses (Ray-Ban Meta), wearables, and Horizon on mobile. VR pivot cost ~$70B in cumulative losses.

5. **AI Monetization Pipeline:** Meta AI (1B MAU) moving toward subscription monetization ("Meta AI+"). Threads scaling to ads globally.

6. **Regulatory Confidence:** Management expressed confidence post-FTC victory. EU regulatory risks acknowledged but not quantified.
