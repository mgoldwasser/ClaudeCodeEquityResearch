# NVIDIA Q4 FY2026 Earnings Call — Key Excerpts
**Date:** 2026-02-25
**Participants:** Jensen Huang (CEO), Colette Kress (CFO)
**Source:** The Motley Fool transcript + Yahoo Finance highlights (Tier 2)
**Retrieval Date:** 2026-03-19

---

## Revenue & Financial Commentary (Colette Kress)

- "Total revenue of $68,000,000,000 was up 73% year over year, accelerating from Q3."
- "Data Center generated revenue of $194,000,000,000 [full year], up 68% year over year."
- "We expect sequential revenue growth throughout calendar 2026, exceeding what was included in the $500,000,000,000 Blackwell and Rubin revenue opportunity."
- "Nearly 9 gigawatts of infrastructure on Blackwell are deployed and consumed by major cloud service providers."
- "Grace Blackwell systems accounted for roughly two-thirds of Data Center revenue in the quarter."
- Networking revenue: "$11,000,000,000...up more than 3.5x year over year."
- Free cash flow: $35B in Q4 and $97B in full fiscal year 2026.
- "$41 billion or 43% of free cash flow returned to shareholders in the form of share repurchases and dividends."

## Supply & Demand Commentary (Colette Kress)

- "We expect supply constraints to be the headwind to Gaming in Q1 and beyond."
- China revenue: "We expect H20 China data center revenue to remain uncertain." NVIDIA excludes China Data Center compute revenue from guidance assumptions.
- H20 context: NVIDIA "still hasn't sold its U.S.-approved China AI chips" as of Q4 earnings call (CNBC, 2026-02-26).

## Strategic Positioning (Jensen Huang)

- "Compute equals revenues" — core thesis on inference value.
- "The ChatGPT moment of agentic AI has arrived."
- "Every country will build and operate some parts of its AI infrastructure." [Sovereign AI thesis]
- "Inference is revenues, that compute equals revenues. Now, in this new world."
- "When you are inferencing at a much higher speed...it directly translates into revenues."

## Customer Concentration

- Top 5 customers: approximately 50% of revenue; growth being led by non-hyperscale segments.
- Expanding customer base: enterprises, AI makers, and sovereign nations.

## Product Roadmap Commentary

- "Vera Rubin samples...shipped...production shipments on track for the second half [of calendar 2026]."
- Rubin promises "10x lower inference costs than Blackwell."
- New products announced: Vera CPU, Rubin GPU, NVLink 6, ConnectX-9 SuperNIC, BlueField-4 DPU, Spectrum-6 Ethernet.

## Q1 FY2027 Guidance

- Revenue: $78.0 billion ± 2%
- Non-GAAP gross margin: 75.0% ± 50 bps
- Non-GAAP operating expenses: ~$7.5B
- Tax rate (FY2027): 17–19%
- Note: Guidance EXCLUDES assumed China Data Center compute revenue.

## GTC 2026 Keynote Excerpts (Jensen Huang — 2026-03-16)

**Source:** CNBC, Tom's Hardware, TechRepublic, QZ.com (Tier 7-8)
**Event:** NVIDIA GTC 2026, San Jose, March 16–19, 2026

- "AI is no longer a single breakthrough or application — it is essential infrastructure."
- Raised revenue opportunity from $500B to $1 trillion through 2027 (Blackwell + Rubin combined orders).
- "Computing demand has risen '1 million times over the last few years'."
- "The inference inflection has arrived" — central thesis of keynote.
- Demand booming from "startups and big companies alike."
- Unveiled Vera Rubin platform: 7 chips, 5 rack-scale systems, 1 AI supercomputer.
- Feynman architecture announced as next-generation after Rubin.
- Agentic AI: OpenClaw open-source framework described as "fastest-growing open-source project in history."
- Physical AI partnerships: BYD, Hyundai, Nissan, Geely (autonomous vehicles); ABB, Universal Robots, KUKA (robotics).
- Uber partnership for robotaxi deployment.

## Key Risk Disclosures (from 10-K per last10k.com summary)

- Supply chain constraints: manufacturing lead times could delay customer deployments.
- Customer concentration: revenue heavily concentrated among major cloud providers.
- Geopolitical risks: export restrictions and tariffs affecting supply chains.
- Macroeconomic factors: tariffs, inflation, interest rates.
- Inventory risk: potential for decreases in product yields and higher material costs.
- Competitive pressure: open-source AI and alternative solutions.
- China uncertainty: H20 chip restricted; domestic Chinese AI chip development accelerating.
