## Regulatory Environment (Critical Risk Factors)

### EU — Digital Markets Act (DMA) Compliance
- April 22, 2025: European Commission fined Meta €200 million for DMA non-compliance
  - Violation: "Pay or consent" model breached prohibition on personal data combination
  - Meta required to offer free alternative with less personalized ads
  - Meta committed to reduced data sharing for EU users beginning January 2026
- February 9, 2026: EC issued Statement of Objections for WhatsApp AI policy
  - Meta banned third-party AI chatbots (ChatGPT, etc.) from WhatsApp Business (Oct 2025 policy change)
  - EC considering "rare interim measures" to force restoration of third-party access
  - Maximum potential fine: Up to 10% of global revenue (~$16B+)
- Source: EC press releases, TLT LLP analysis

### EU — GDPR Historical Fine
- 2023: Meta fined €1.2 billion for GDPR violation (data transfer to US)
- DMA oversight now primary regulatory framework for EU going forward
- Source: GDPR Local

### US — FTC Antitrust Case RESOLVED
- November 18, 2025: Judge James Boasberg ruled FOR Meta
  - Court found FTC failed to prove Meta holds monopoly in "personal social networking"
  - Expanded market definition includes TikTok and YouTube (eliminating narrow market argument)
  - Meta will NOT be required to spin off Instagram or WhatsApp
- Key takeaway: Major overhang REMOVED. Instagram and WhatsApp remain with Meta.
- Source: NPR, Bloomberg, Axios

### US — Youth Mental Health Litigation
- Multiple lawsuits alleging Meta platforms intentionally designed to harm younger users
- CFO (Q4 2025): Trials scheduled for 2026 "may ultimately result in a material loss"
- Potential outcomes: Financial penalties, mandatory product changes, reduced youth engagement
- No settlement or resolution announced as of March 2026

### China Market Access
- Meta's services remain banned in China
- Represents significant TAM exclusion (~1.4B population)
- Some monetization from Chinese advertisers targeting non-China users (Temu, Shein, etc.)

### US Political/Regulatory (2025-2026)
- Meta withdrew from DEI programs (Jan 2025), aligned politically with Trump administration
- Reduced content moderation policies
- Regulatory risk somewhat reduced under current US administration
- FTC lawsuit withdrawal suggests reduced US regulatory pressure vs. prior administration

### India Market Opportunity
- Meta AI's largest market: ~300M users (30% of global Meta AI user base)
- WhatsApp dominant in India (>500M users)
- India represents high-growth, lower-monetization market

## AI Infrastructure Competition & Geopolitics
- DeepSeek (China): Jan 2025 R1 model demonstrated efficient AI; raised questions about US AI infrastructure spending rationality
- US export controls on advanced chips to China: Limits competition from Chinese AI; also limits Meta's ability to operate in China
- Compute arms race: Meta, Google, Microsoft, Amazon all accelerating infrastructure investment
- Nuclear power deals: Meta signed 6.6GW nuclear deal with Vistra/TerraPower for data centers
- 5GW "Hyperion" campus in Louisiana planned

## Key Macro Risk Scenarios
1. **Recession risk:** ~15-25% digital ad revenue decline in severe recession; Meta highly leveraged to ad cycle
2. **EU regulatory escalation:** Maximum €16B fine (DMA) + potential forced product changes in EU (~23% of revenue)
3. **AI infrastructure write-down risk:** If AI ROI does not materialize, $115-135B 2026 CapEx could become stranded assets
4. **FX headwind reversal:** USD strength could reverse the 4% Q1 tailwind
5. **Geopolitical:** US-China trade tensions affect Chinese advertiser spending (Temu/Shein were >$7B in Meta ad revenue)
