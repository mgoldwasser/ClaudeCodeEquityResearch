## Key Risk Factors (Section 1A)

1. **Supply Chain Constraints** — Manufacturing lead times and capacity limitations could delay customer deployments and reduce AI adoption scale. HBM and CoWoS packaging capacity are fully allocated through 2026.

2. **Customer Concentration** — Revenue heavily concentrated among major cloud service providers (CSPs). Top 5 customers represent ~50% of revenue. Loss of any major CSP could have material impact.

3. **Geopolitical / Export Control Risks** — U.S. export restrictions on H20 chips to China resulted in $4.5B inventory charge in Q1 FY2026. China once represented ~20%+ of data center revenue. Ongoing uncertainty around H20 licensing.

4. **Macroeconomic Factors** — Tariffs, inflation, interest rates impact operations and customer demand.

5. **Inventory Risk** — Decreases in product yields and higher material costs could impair margins. Rapid product transitions (Hopper→Blackwell→Rubin) create inventory obsolescence risk.

6. **Competitive Pressure** — AMD MI300X/MI400 series, Google TPUs, Amazon Trainium/Inferentia, custom ASICs from hyperscalers, and open-source software tools reducing CUDA dependency.

7. **Capex Investment Risk** — $17.5B committed to infrastructure investment in FY2026; capital-light fabless model means reliance on TSMC creates concentration risk.

8. **Regulatory Risk** — Antitrust scrutiny growing globally as NVIDIA approaches dominant market position. EU and other regulators have opened investigations.
