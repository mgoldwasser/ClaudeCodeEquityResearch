## Key Risk Factors (from 10-K FY2025, Item 1A)

### Regulatory & Legal Risks
1. **EU Digital Markets Act (DMA):** Ongoing compliance requirements; €200M fine paid April 2025; WhatsApp AI investigation ongoing with potential for interim measures and fines up to 10% of global revenue
2. **EU GDPR:** €1.2B fine (2023); ongoing data localization and transfer restrictions
3. **Youth mental health litigation:** CFO: Trials in 2026 "may ultimately result in a material loss"; multiple state/federal lawsuits
4. **Content moderation liability:** Section 230 changes could impose liability for user-generated content
5. **Antitrust (US):** FTC case resolved in Meta's favor (Nov 2025); limited near-term US antitrust risk

### Technology & Competition Risks
6. **AI infrastructure ROI:** $115-135B 2026 CapEx with uncertain return timeline; risk of stranded assets if AI monetization disappoints
7. **Llama model competitiveness:** Llama 4 received "tepid response from developers"; Avocado (Llama successor) in testing; risk of falling behind OpenAI, Google, Anthropic
8. **Apple ATT privacy:** Ongoing impact on ad targeting; partially mitigated by on-platform signal improvement
9. **TikTok competition:** TikTok ban/divestiture uncertainty; Meta stands to benefit from ban but outcome unclear

### Operational Risks
10. **Reality Labs losses:** $19.2B operating loss in 2025; losses expected to remain at 2025 levels in 2026; cumulative >$70B since 2020
11. **Founder control risk:** Zuckerberg retains effective majority control via Class B shares; minority shareholder interests may not be aligned
12. **Data center power:** Nuclear and other power agreements needed for massive compute buildout; execution risk in construction timelines
13. **Talent:** Competition for AI talent; $14.3B investment in Scale AI acquisition noted

## Capital Structure Details

- Total long-term debt: $58,744M

- Note: Meta has significantly increased leverage to fund AI infrastructure
