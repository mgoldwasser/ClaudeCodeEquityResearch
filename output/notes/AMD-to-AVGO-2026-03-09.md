## Observation

Custom AI ASIC shipments are growing 44.6% in 2026, significantly outpacing GPU growth of 16.1%. Broadcom commands 60-80% of the AI ASIC market through partnerships with Google (TPU), Meta, and OpenAI. However, AMD's mega-deals with OpenAI and Meta (12 GW combined, ~$200B potential revenue) demonstrate that even hyperscalers building custom ASICs still require GPU infrastructure for training and general-purpose AI workloads. OpenAI is simultaneously building custom chips with Broadcom while committing 6 GW to AMD GPUs.

## Implication for Target

Broadcom's AI ASIC revenue trajectory remains strong, but the "custom ASICs will replace GPUs" narrative is more nuanced than market consensus. The AMD mega-deals suggest GPUs and ASICs serve complementary roles — GPUs for training/general inference, ASICs for optimized inference at massive scale. Broadcom benefits from sector TAM expansion but should not be modeled as a GPU replacement story. The custom ASIC market is projected to grow from $28B to $150B by 2030 (base case, 40% CAGR), with Broadcom capturing 60%+ of that opportunity.
