## Observation

AMD's server CPU revenue share reached 41% in Q2 2025 and unit share hit 36-40% by Q4 2025, up from 2% in 2017. EPYC Turin delivers up to 40% better performance vs. Intel Xeon in 2P configurations. AMD is gaining 6-8pp of server CPU share per year. Industry assessments describe Intel as "more than one generation behind" in server CPUs. AMD now has ~1,600 cloud instances (+50% YoY) and its revenue share exceeding unit share indicates AMD is winning the highest-margin server segments. In desktop CPUs, AMD's revenue share reached 42.6% (+14.6pp YoY), and AMD now outsells Intel in premium desktop segments.

## Implication for Target

Intel faces continued server CPU share erosion that could reach parity (50/50) by late 2026. The financial impact is severe: server CPUs are Intel's highest-margin business, and losing share in 2P/4P high-performance segments (where AMD is over-indexing on revenue share) disproportionately compresses Intel's profitability. Intel's Diamond Rapids must deliver competitive performance to arrest share loss, but process delays (18A timeline uncertain) suggest Intel may remain architecturally behind through at least 2027. Additionally, Intel's Altera spin-out (51% to Silver Lake at $8.75B) removes a competitive lever against AMD's Xilinx FPGA business while raising capital — a defensive move, not an offensive one.
