## Sector Analyst — AMD Brief

**Bottom Line:** AMD is positioned in the right markets (AI accelerators + server CPUs are fastest-growing sub-sectors), gaining share in concentrated markets as #2 player, and operating in the highest-margin value chain position (fabless design at 57% GM) — but the single most important constraint is TSMC CoWoS capacity allocation, not demand.

**Key Numbers:**
- AI GPU TAM: $140B (2025) → $378B (2030 base, 22% CAGR); Custom ASIC TAM: $28B → $151B (40% CAGR) [ESTIMATED]
- Server CPU TAM: $42B (2025) → $62B (2030 base, 8% CAGR) [ESTIMATED]
- Global semi market: $772B (2025) → $975B (2026, +25%) [Source: WSTS — Tier 5]
- AMD AI GPU share: 10% (2025) → 13.9% (2027) → 18.8% (2030, base case) [Source: Sector share model]
- AMD Server CPU share: 34.6% → 42.0% (2027) → 48.9% (2030, base case)
- TSMC CoWoS: ~80,000 wafers/month, >100% utilization; NVIDIA ~60% allocation, AMD ~8-10% [Source: TrendForce, Digitimes — Tier 3]
- HHI: AI GPU 6,864 / Server CPU 4,609 / Client PC 5,378 — all highly concentrated
- AMD ROIC: ~15% [ESTIMATED] vs. WACC ~11% — marginal value creator (Xilinx goodwill drag)

**Critical Assumptions:**
- AI accelerator adoption at Early Majority (~30% enterprise penetration), 3-4 years of high-growth remaining [ASSUMPTION]
- AMD share gains assume current product cadence continues (Turin → Venice, MI350 → MI450 → MI500) [ASSUMPTION]
- TAM estimates have historical tendency to overstate by 20-40% in growth sectors [ASSUMPTION]
- TSMC expands CoWoS to 130,000 wafers/month by late 2026 [Source: TrendForce — Tier 3] [confidence: medium]

**Risks Identified:**
- TSMC CoWoS capacity: AMD's 12 GW mega-deal commitments may exceed TSMC allocation — supply-constrained, not demand-constrained
- CUDA software moat limits AMD AI GPU ceiling to ~20-25% without step-function ROCm improvement
- Custom ASIC growth (44.6% 2026) could erode GPU TAM; GPUs and ASICs coexist but ratio may shift
- Regulatory environment net negative: export controls cost more than CHIPS Act subsidies for fabless companies

**Weakest Point:** The supply-side constraint (TSMC CoWoS allocation at ~8-10% vs. NVIDIA's ~60%) may limit AMD's AI GPU ramp regardless of demand — NVIDIA has pre-booked incremental capacity.
