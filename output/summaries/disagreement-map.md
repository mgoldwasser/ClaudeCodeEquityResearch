## Cross-Critique Disagreement Map — AMD
Date: 2026-03-09

**Resolved Agreements:**
- Balance sheet is an unambiguous strength: 14/14 analysts agree. Net cash $7.25B, A/A1 rated, 59x interest coverage. Failure will be execution-driven, not solvency-driven.
- MI450 execution is the single highest-impact uncertainty: 13/14 agree (Credit Analyst notes it's not a credit risk). H2 2026 is the most consequential period in AMD's history.
- CUDA/ROCm software gap is the structural constraint on GPU share: 12/14 agree. CUDA's 18-year ecosystem (6M developers) creates 30-99% effective performance advantage across benchmarks.
- Warrant dilution (320M shares, ~10-20%) is material and under-modeled: 11/14 agree. ESG Analyst's 10.8% probability-weighted dilution adopted as team standard.
- GPU revenue non-disclosure is a transparency concern: 10/14 agree. AMD refuses to break out Instinct GPU revenue from Data Center segment.
- Data completeness is ~55-65%: 12/14 agree. Tool failures on historical prices, options, competitor financials, and proxy statement cascaded across analyses.

**Active Disputes (Partially Resolved):**
- **WACC/Beta:** DCF used 16% (beta 2.02); Credit/Catalyst/Sector Analysts argued 13-14% is appropriate for forward-looking analysis. DCF Analyst partially accepted: adopted declining schedule 16%→14%. Impact: $60-70/share swing. Rebuttal outcome: partially resolved — base case shifted from $155 to $175-190.
- **Mega-deal realization rate:** DCF started at 75%, Competitive/Devil's Advocate argued 50-60%. Competitive Analyst settled at 60-70%. DCF revised to 65%. Partially resolved.
- **MI450 on-time probability:** Catalyst started at 75%, multiple analysts challenged with SemiAnalysis delay report. Revised to 60-65%. Technical Analyst argues price action implies 40-50%. Partially resolved.
- **Bear case floor:** Devil's Advocate started at $80, multiple analysts argued balance sheet + non-AI business sets floor at $120-140. DA revised to $120-140. Resolved.
- **Bear probability:** Devil's Advocate at 30-35%, Risk Analyst revised to 28-30%, Sector Analyst argues joint probability math yields 10-15%. Unresolved — range is 15-30%.
- **AI capex sustainability:** Macro argues capex-to-revenue ratio of 15-25:1 is historically unprecedented; Catalyst/Sector counter that denominator should include $500B+ enterprise IT. Partially resolved — ratio revised but still elevated.

**Unresolved Contradictions:**
1. **Fair value range:** DCF ($175-190 revised), Quant ($184-190 revised), Risk ($175-210 revised), Devil's Advocate ($120-140 bear / $178 EV). No consensus price target — range spans $120-210.
2. **Bridge contract hypothesis:** Devil's Advocate argues mega-deals are temporary while custom ASICs mature (OpenAI's Broadcom deal is 67% larger). Sector/Catalyst counter that GPU/ASIC deployment is complementary, not substitutional. No resolution — this is the thesis's key unresolved risk.
3. **ROIC vs WACC:** Devil's Advocate flags GAAP ROIC of 6.6% below any WACC estimate (value destruction). Sector argues operational ROIC of 12-15% is the relevant metric. Unreconciled.
4. **Cycle timing:** Macro says 6-18 months of growth remaining; Sector says 3-4 years. Technical Analyst notes AMD peaked Oct 2025 while AI news continued — classic late-cycle divergence. Unresolved.

**Strongest Critiques (by impact):**
1. **WACC at 16% is too high** (Credit → DCF): Partially accepted, $60-70/share impact. The single largest valuation driver.
2. **Bridge contract risk** (Devil's Advocate → all): Not resolved. If mega-deals are bridge contracts, bull case collapses.
3. **MI450 delay probability understated** (Technical/Risk → Catalyst): Partially accepted, reduced to 60-65% on-time from 75%.
4. **GPU revenue opacity** (Forensic/Sentiment → all): Accepted. The primary thesis driver has no Tier 1 financial disclosure.
5. **Trailing PEG misleading** (Macro/Competitive → Quant): Accepted. Forward PEG of 0.82-1.0x eliminates "deep value" signal.
