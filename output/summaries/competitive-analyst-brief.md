## Competitive Analyst — AMD Brief

**Bottom Line:** AMD holds a strong and widening moat in server CPUs (approaching parity with Intel) but its AI GPU business — the primary valuation driver — operates from a clear #2 position behind NVIDIA with a significant CUDA software ecosystem disadvantage; overall competitive score 6/10.

**Key Numbers:**
- Server CPU share: 36-40% unit / 41% revenue (vs. Intel 60-64% unit) [Source: Mercury Research, Fusion Worldwide — Tier 3-6]
- AI GPU share: ~8-12%, gained 2.6pp in Q4 2025; NVIDIA >80% [Source: TechNetBooks — Tier 6]
- CUDA Gap Score: 28.7-99.1 across benchmarks (30-99% effective NVIDIA performance advantage) [Source: AIM Multiple — Tier 6]
- Custom ASIC growth: 44.6% in 2026 vs. GPU 16.1% [Source: CNBC — Tier 3-6]
- AI GPU TAM: ~$200-250B in 2026 [ESTIMATED]; AMD SAM ~$170-210B (excl. internal ASICs)
- MI450 specs: 432 GB HBM4, 1.4 exaFLOPS FP8, 50% more memory than NVIDIA Vera Rubin [Source: SemiAnalysis, Tom's Hardware — Tier 3-6]
- OpenAI + Meta: 12 GW combined, ~$200B potential revenue [Source: AMD IR — Tier 1]

**Critical Assumptions:**
- NVIDIA's CUDA moat (6M developers, 300+ libraries, 18 years) can be narrowed by ROCm + Triton/MLIR abstraction [confidence: low]
- Custom ASICs and GPUs are complementary, not purely substitutional [confidence: medium]
- MI450 delivers claimed 50% memory advantage over Vera Rubin at production scale [confidence: medium]
- EPYC server share can reach 45-50% without triggering aggressive Intel pricing response [confidence: medium-high]

**Risks Identified:**
- #1 threat: NVIDIA CUDA ecosystem + custom ASIC cannibalization (dual squeeze); 60% probability in 3 years
- If materialized: -$5-15B vs. bull case by 2028; stock impact -30-50%
- AMD AI GPU share may plateau at 12-15% rather than reaching 20-25%
- ROCm developer base is a fraction of CUDA's — gap compounds over time via power laws

**Weakest Point:** AI GPU moat durability rated only 5/10 — the highest-value business has the narrowest moat, and mega-deals may be bridge contracts rather than permanent demand.
