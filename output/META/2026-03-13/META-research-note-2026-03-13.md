## 3. Business Overview

Meta operates the world's largest social media ecosystem — Facebook (3.3B MAU), Instagram (2.4B MAU), WhatsApp (3.3B+ users), Messenger, and Threads (400M MAU) — collectively reaching 3.58B daily active people globally. The company generates 98% of revenue ($196B in FY2025) from digital advertising across its Family of Apps (FoA) segment, monetizing attention through a real-time ad auction processing billions of impressions per second for 10M+ advertisers. `[Source: Meta Q4/FY2025 earnings press release, Tier 1]`

Two developments define the current thesis. First, AI has transformed the core advertising engine: Advantage+ automated ad tools reached a $60B annual run rate by Q3 2025, driving simultaneous ad impression growth (+12% FY) and price-per-ad increases (+9% FY) — a Jevons paradox dynamic where better targeting creates both more volume and higher prices. `[Source: Q3 2025 earnings call, Tier 1]` Second, Meta is executing the largest single-year capital deployment in corporate history: $115-135B in FY2026 CapEx for AI infrastructure, including custom MTIA v3 "Iris" chips, a 5GW "Hyperion" data center campus, and 6.6GW of contracted nuclear power. `[Source: Q4 2025 earnings guidance, Tier 1]`

Reality Labs — the AR/VR hardware segment — remains a material capital drain: $2.2B in FY2025 revenue against $19.2B in operating losses. Cumulative RL losses exceed $70B since 2020. Management has pivoted from VR/metaverse toward AI-integrated glasses (Ray-Ban Meta), but the segment remains fundamentally unprofitable with no disclosed path to breakeven. `[Source: Meta FY2025 earnings, Tier 1]`
