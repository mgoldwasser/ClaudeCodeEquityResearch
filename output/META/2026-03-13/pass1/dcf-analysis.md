### CapEx Normalization Thesis

The CapEx trajectory is the single most important variable in this DCF. My base case assumes:

1. **FY2026 = peak CapEx year** at $125B (48.9% of revenue) — management has explicitly guided this
2. **FY2027 = first normalization year** at $105B — growth CapEx declines as Phase 1 AI clusters come online
3. **FY2028-2030 = steady decline** as growth CapEx moderates while maintenance CapEx grows with asset base
4. **Terminal CapEx/revenue = 16%** — above historical but reflects permanent higher infrastructure intensity for AI

If CapEx does NOT normalize (stays at 40%+ of revenue through FY2028), the base case fair value drops by ~35%. See sensitivity table.

## 5. Terminal Value

### Method 1: Perpetuity Growth (Base Case)

- Terminal FCF (FY2030 UFCF × (1+g)): $146,000M × 1.03 = $150,380M
- Terminal Growth Rate: 3.0% `[ASSUMPTION: GDP growth (~2.5%) + modest pricing power (~0.5%). Digital advertising growing structurally above GDP due to continued shift from traditional media. Below Meta's 5Y historical CAGR of 18.5% by wide margin — conservative for a company with platform-level network effects.]`
- Terminal Value = $150,380M / (10.4% - 3.0%) = $150,380M / 7.4% = **$2,032,162M**

### Method 2: Exit Multiple

- Terminal EBITDA (FY2030): $248,600M
- Exit Multiple: 14.0x `[ASSUMPTION: Based on mature large-cap tech trading range of 12-18x EBITDA; Meta's dominant market position and high margins support mid-range multiple. Comparable: Google at maturity]`
- Terminal Value = $248,600M × 14.0x = **$3,480,400M**

### Averaged Terminal Value (Base Case)

- Perpetuity Growth TV: $2,032,162M
- Exit Multiple TV: $3,480,400M
- **Weighted Average TV (60% perpetuity / 40% exit multiple): $2,611,457M**

`[ASSUMPTION: Overweight perpetuity method (60/40) as it is less sensitive to multiple assumptions and more appropriate for a 5-year explicit forecast. The exit multiple method yields significantly higher value, suggesting the perpetuity terminal growth rate may be conservative OR the exit multiple is too generous.]`

**Implied terminal multiple cross-check:** Perpetuity TV of $2,032B / Terminal EBITDA of $248.6B = 8.2x implied exit EBITDA. This is low for a business with 58% EBITDA margins and 3% perpetuity growth, but reasonable given the AI CapEx uncertainty embedded in the forecast.

### Probability Justification

The 25/50/25 weighting reflects genuine uncertainty about the AI CapEx cycle outcome. The base case at 50% represents the most likely path — management has delivered on operational targets consistently since the 2023 "Year of Efficiency," supporting credibility on the operating income commitment. The bull case at 25% is justified by Meta's track record of monetizing new surfaces (Stories, Reels) and the structural tailwind if TikTok faces US restrictions. The bear case at 25% is elevated (vs. a typical 15-20%) because: (1) the $125B CapEx commitment is unprecedented in scale and has no proven ROI timeline, (2) digital advertising is cyclical and has not been through a recession during the AI spending era, and (3) EU regulatory escalation creates a credible path to 10%+ revenue haircut in Europe.
