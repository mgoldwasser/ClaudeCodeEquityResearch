## Section 1: Company Overview

**Company:** Meta Platforms, Inc.
**Ticker:** META (NASDAQ)
**Headquarters:** Menlo Park, California
**Founded:** 2004 (as Facebook); renamed Meta Platforms 2021
**Fiscal Year End:** December 31
**Reporting Currency:** USD

**Business Description:**
Meta Platforms operates the world's largest social media ecosystem: Facebook (~3.3B MAU), Instagram (~2.4B MAU), WhatsApp (3.3B+ users), Messenger, and Threads (400M MAU as of Aug 2025). The company generates ~98% of revenue from digital advertising across its "Family of Apps." A second segment, Reality Labs, develops augmented/virtual reality hardware (Quest, Ray-Ban Meta glasses, Oakley Meta Vanguards) and software and has been unprofitable, generating cumulative losses exceeding $70B since 2020.

Meta is undergoing a strategic transformation: from a social media advertising company to an AI infrastructure and services platform. CEO Zuckerberg frames the company's mission as building "Personal Superintelligence" and has committed $115-135B in capital expenditures for 2026 to front-load AI compute capacity.

**Segments:**
1. **Family of Apps (FoA):** Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, Threads — 98% of revenue from advertising
2. **Reality Labs (RL):** VR/AR hardware and software — $2.2B revenue, $19.2B operating loss in FY2025

   November 18, 2025: Judge Boasberg ruled that FTC failed to prove monopoly power. Market expanded to include TikTok/YouTube. Instagram and WhatsApp remain with Meta permanently.
   Source: Federal court ruling (Tier 1)

### Active / Material
2. **EU DMA — WhatsApp AI Investigation (February 9, 2026):**

## Section 10: Bear Case Evidence (Documented)

1. **CapEx ROI Uncertainty:** $115-135B 2026 CapEx with no quantified ROI timeline. FCF compression to ~$25-35B in 2026E (from $43.6B in FY2025). Unlike Google Cloud or AWS, Meta lacks proven B2B cloud revenue stream to monetize excess compute.

2. **Llama Model Gap:** Llama 4 launched to tepid developer reception (spring 2025). Avocado (Llama 5) in testing but unreleased. Risk: OpenAI, Google, Anthropic maintain competitive advantage in frontier AI models.

3. **EU Regulatory Escalation Pattern:** Three major EU enforcement actions in 2 years (GDPR €1.2B, DMA €200M, DMA WhatsApp investigation). WhatsApp AI investigation could result in €16B+ fine or forced interoperability harming competitive moat.

4. **Reality Labs Permanent Capital Destruction:** $19.2B annual operating loss, >$70B cumulative. Management no longer mentions "metaverse." Pivot to AI glasses from VR headsets; Quest declining; Ray-Ban glasses growing but trivially small at current scale. Management explicitly guiding losses to "remain at 2025 levels" in 2026.

5. **Advertising Cyclicality:** Digital ad revenue declined in 2022 (~-1% for Meta). In a recession, a 15-25% revenue decline = -$30-50B; amplified by fixed AI infrastructure costs.

6. **Youth Mental Health Liability:** 2026 trial calendar; CFO flagged material loss risk; no financial provision estimable currently.

7. **Governance:** Zuckerberg retains majority control via Class B shares. $135B CapEx commitment without shareholder approval. Historical: VR/metaverse pivot cost shareholders estimated >$500B in market value before reversal.

8. **Chinese Advertiser Concentration:** Temu, Shein, and other Chinese e-commerce players were estimated at >$7B in Meta ad spend; US-China trade tensions could reduce this contribution.
