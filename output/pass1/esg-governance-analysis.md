**Dilution Scenario Analysis:**

| Scenario | Shares if Warrants Vest | Dilution to Existing Shareholders | Implied Conditions |
|----------|------------------------|----------------------------------|-------------------|
| No warrants vest | 1,636M | 0% | Deals fail / AMD misses milestones |

| 100% vest | 1,956M | ~19.6% | Full 12 GW deployment + $600 stock price |

**Governance Assessment of Warrant Issuance:**

This is the single most material governance issue at AMD. Key concerns:

1. **No shareholder vote.** Under NASDAQ listing rules, AMD may have relied on the "inducement grant" exception or determined the warrants qualified as commercial arrangements rather than equity compensation plans requiring shareholder approval. However, issuing warrants representing ~20% of shares outstanding at $0.01/share to two customers — without a shareholder vote — is a governance process concern regardless of legal compliance. [DATA GAP: Exact NASDAQ rule relied upon not confirmed.]

2. **Precedent risk.** If AMD is "buying" market share with equity, future deals could follow the same template, creating open-ended dilution risk. Management has not disclosed a cap on total warrant commitments.

3. **Alignment defense.** AMD argues the warrants align incentives: customers only get equity if they deploy AMD GPUs at scale AND the stock reaches $600 (3x current price). If the stock reaches $600 with 320M additional shares, the market cap would be ~$1.17T — meaning the warrant dilution is a ~$192B cost against ~$200B+ in deal revenue. On an NPV basis, analysts estimate this is roughly an even swap. [Source: Tom's Hardware, The Decoder — Tier 3, retrieved 2026-03-09]

4. **Full vesting probability.** Full vesting requires $600 stock price by 2031, which requires ~26% CAGR from $190 over 5 years. This is aggressive but not impossible given management's >$20 EPS target. Partial vesting at lower thresholds is more likely, limiting actual dilution to ~5-10%.

## 9. ESG Impact on Valuation

| ESG Factor | Valuation Impact | Channel | Magnitude |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|-----------|
| Governance quality (board) | Slightly Negative | Combined Chair/CEO marginally increases governance risk premium | -5 to -10bps on WACC |
| Warrant dilution governance | **Negative** | Potential 0-19.6% equity dilution without shareholder vote | -2% to -5% of equity value (probability-weighted) |
| SBC dilution (regular) | Neutral | Offset by buybacks; net dilution ~0% | ~0% |
| Environmental liability | Neutral | Fabless model; indirect TSMC exposure priced into supply chain risk | ~0% |
| Social risk (talent) | Slightly Negative | Key-person risk (Lisa Su); rising SBC costs | -5bps on WACC |
| ESG reputation | Slightly Positive | Strong ESG scores may attract ESG-mandated capital flows | +5bps (marginal) |
| **Net ESG adjustment** | **Slightly Negative** | | **-10 to -15bps on WACC; -2% to -5% probability-weighted warrant dilution** |

**Should the DCF include an ESG risk premium?**

Yes, but modest. Suggest adding **10-15bps** to the company-specific risk premium in WACC to reflect: (1) combined Chair/CEO governance structure, (2) warrant governance process risk, and (3) key-person dependency on Lisa Su. This results in ~10-15bps higher WACC and approximately 1.5-2.5% lower fair value.

Separately, the warrant dilution should be modeled as a **direct equity adjustment** rather than through WACC: probability-weight the dilution scenarios (0% vesting: 30% probability; 50% vesting: 45% probability; 100% vesting: 25% probability) and subtract the expected dilution from the per-share fair value. Expected dilution: (0.30 x 0%) + (0.45 x 9.8%) + (0.25 x 19.6%) = **9.3% expected dilution to existing shareholders.**

## ESG & Governance Summary

AMD's governance is above-average for large-cap semiconductors: the board is independent (87.5%), diverse (37.5% female, 50% racially diverse), recently refreshed with KC McClure's appointment, and has overseen one of the most impressive corporate transformations in tech history under Lisa Su. Shareholder rights are strong — no dual-class shares, no poison pill, annual director elections, proxy access, and majority voting. Executive compensation at ~$31M is elevated but shows genuine pay-for-performance linkage (72.2% EIP payout in FY2024 despite record revenue). Regular SBC dilution (~0.7-1.0% gross annually) is fully offset by the $14B buyback program, resulting in net zero dilution from employee compensation.

**The single biggest ESG/governance risk is the 320M warrant shares issued to OpenAI and Meta at $0.01/share without shareholder approval.** This represents up to 19.6% potential dilution and was approved solely at the board level. While the warrants are performance-gated (deployment milestones + $600 stock price by 2031) and arguably NPV-neutral against ~$200B in deal revenue, the governance process — issuing ~20% of outstanding equity to two customers without a shareholder vote — sets a concerning precedent. If AMD signs additional "equity-for-demand" deals, cumulative dilution risk could become material. The probability-weighted expected dilution is ~9.3%, which should be modeled as a direct equity value adjustment.

ESG factors warrant a modest valuation adjustment: +10-15bps to WACC for governance structure risk, plus a 9.3% probability-weighted dilution haircut to per-share value from warrant exposure. No environmental or social risks are material enough to warrant additional valuation adjustment beyond what is captured in standard supply chain and key-person risk analysis.

**Near-term governance catalysts:** (1) 2026 proxy season — watch for shareholder proposals on warrant approval requirements or special meeting threshold removal; (2) any additional "equity-for-demand" customer deals would compound dilution concerns; (3) Lisa Su compensation say-on-pay vote (likely passes given track record, but warrant governance may attract protest votes).
