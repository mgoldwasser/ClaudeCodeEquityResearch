## Information Edge Assessment

| Question | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| Does the market fully understand this business? | **Mostly, but two key blindspots exist.** (1) The real-world performance gap between AMD benchmarks and production deployments is poorly understood — AMD claims 20-40% advantage over B200 in specific benchmarks, but production software stack performance may differ materially. (2) The warrant dilution economics: 320M shares at $0.01 is effectively free equity, but full vesting requires $600 stock price + deployment milestones, creating a convexity the market hasn't fully modeled |
| Are sell-side estimates well-calibrated? | **Systematically too low on revenue, too high on price targets.** AMD has beaten consensus revenue in each of the last 4 quarters by 3-6%. The average PT of $261 implies 37% upside, but the median PT of $280 is more informative. The $238 spread between low ($120) and high ($358) PTs reflects genuine disagreement about execution risk |
| Is there an upcoming data point that could surprise? | **Yes — MI450 real-world deployment benchmarks.** When Oracle, OpenAI, or Meta first deploy MI450 at scale in Q3 2026 and publish (or leak) comparative performance data, this will be the most consequential data point. If MI450 delivers on AMD's claims (50% more memory than Vera Rubin, competitive performance), the competitive narrative shifts permanently |
| Is the company under-followed? (< 10 analysts) | **No — 34 analysts cover AMD.** Among the most-followed semiconductor stocks globally |
| Has management recently guided expectations? | **Yes — Q1 2026 guidance on 2026-02-03:** Revenue ~$9.8B (+/-$300M), ~32% YoY growth, 55% non-GAAP GM. Long-term targets: DC >60% CAGR, >35% total revenue CAGR, >$20 EPS |
| Is there insider activity worth noting? | **Neutral.** Lisa Su's selling is 100% via 10b5-1 pre-programmed plans (sold $26.8M in Feb 2026, ~45K shares Dec 2025). She retains ~3.15M shares (~$600M at current price). No discretionary selling signal |

**Information edge summary:**

The primary information edge in AMD is the gap between announced deals and proven execution. The market knows about the $200B+ OpenAI/Meta pipeline, the MI450 specs, and the EPYC share gains. What the market does NOT know — and cannot know until H2 2026 — is whether AMD can execute at hyperscale for the first time in its history. AMD has never shipped at GW scale. It has never managed a supply chain for 50,000+ GPUs in a single supercluster. It has never had to prove ROCm at production scale against CUDA. The SemiAnalysis delay report, and AMD's aggressive denial, illustrate the informational vacuum. The resolution of this uncertainty in H2 2026 is the largest potential re-rating event.

A secondary edge exists in the warrant dilution modeling. Most sell-side models treat the 320M warrants as a simple dilution event, but the structure is more nuanced: warrants vest on both deployment milestones AND stock price thresholds ($600). At current $190, the $600 trigger seems distant — but if the deals execute and AMD reaches $400-500, the market will abruptly reprice the dilution. This creates a non-linear risk that accelerates as the stock rises — a "dilution trap" that could cap upside around $350-400 until the market fully digests the warrant overhang.

A third edge is in the CUDA/ROCm competitive gap. The market broadly knows "CUDA is better," but the magnitude varies enormously by workload (28.7% to 99.1% effective performance advantage per the data intelligence memo). If AMD can narrow this gap in key inference workloads (where Meta's deal focuses), the competitive narrative shifts faster than the market expects. ZLUDA's ability to run unmodified CUDA binaries at 80-95% performance is under-appreciated.
