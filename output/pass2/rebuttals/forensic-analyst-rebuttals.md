## Response to Catalyst Analyst

**Critique: $306M reserve release is a legitimate reversal tied to MI308 export license resolution — the timing and causation are clear, not discretionary earnings management.**

Partially accept. The timing correlation between the MI308 export license approval and the Q4 reserve release is supportive of legitimacy, and the CFO's proactive disclosure on the earnings call is a positive transparency signal. However, I maintain "medium-high" confidence (not "high") because: (1) the causation link is inferred from timing, not disclosed explicitly in SEC filings; (2) the DEF 14A was not retrieved, preventing verification of Audit Committee review; (3) the magnitude ($306M, ~300bps gross margin impact) is large enough that discretionary timing would be material. I revise to acknowledge the catalyst context supports legitimacy while maintaining the reserve release as a monitoring item — Q1 FY2026 guided gross margin of 55% will reveal the true run-rate absent the reserve effect.

**Critique: Segment restructuring (Client + Gaming combined) is immaterial — Gaming is <5% of revenue. The real forensic concern is GPU revenue opacity within Data Center.**

Accept. The Catalyst Analyst correctly prioritizes the disclosure concerns. Gaming revenue is declining and immaterial to the thesis. The far more significant forensic question is why AMD does not break out Instinct GPU revenue from the $16.6B Data Center segment. I revise the forensic emphasis: the Client + Gaming combination is noted as a minor concern (standard de-emphasis of declining business), while GPU revenue non-disclosure within Data Center is elevated to the primary forensic concern. The inability to independently verify GPU-specific growth rates, margins, and customer concentration is a genuine obstacle to thesis validation.

**Critique: Forward-looking forensic risk from mega-deal revenue recognition and warrant accounting should be modeled.**

Accept. The forensic analysis is backward-looking by design (M-Score, Z-Score, CFO/NI ratios all use historical data). The Catalyst Analyst correctly identifies that the primary forensic risk going forward is accounting complexity from the mega-deals. I add a forward forensic risk assessment: FY2026-2028 accounting quality risk is 3/5 (elevated) due to: (1) multi-year revenue recognition for framework agreements under ASC 606; (2) warrant fair value measurement at each reporting period (ASC 815 or ASC 718); (3) potential milestone-based revenue timing that creates management discretion. The current 4/5 score applies to historical financials; the forward-looking 3/5 is a new flag for the Director.

## Response to DCF Analyst

**Critique: Altman Z-Score of 14.38 is not diagnostic for a net-cash company with a $310B market cap — the score is inflated by the market cap/total liabilities ratio (X4 = 22.29).**

Accept. The Altman Z-Score was designed for manufacturing companies with meaningful leverage and does not provide useful discrimination for asset-light, net-cash companies like AMD. The score is mechanically inflated by the X4 component (market cap $310B / total liabilities $13.9B = 22.29), which dominates the composite. I maintain the Z-Score calculation for completeness but revise the assessment to: "Z-Score of 14.38 confirms zero bankruptcy risk but is not diagnostic for a net-cash, high-market-cap company. The more informative solvency metrics are: net cash position ($6.55B), interest coverage (implied >30x from operating income of $3.69B vs. ~$120M interest expense), and FCF to total debt ($6.7B FCF / $4.0B debt = 1.7x). All confirm robust solvency."

## Response to Quant Analyst

**Critique: GAAP/Non-GAAP EPS gap of $1.52/share ($2.65 vs. $4.17) is large but the Forensic Analyst should quantify how much of the gap persists in FY2026+ as Xilinx amortization declines.**

Accept. The $2.25B annual Xilinx amortization has an estimated 10-15 year useful life from the February 2022 acquisition close. By FY2026, approximately 4 years of amortization will have elapsed, with $16-17B of the original ~$21B intangible balance already amortized (declining from $21.4B in FY2023 to ~$14B estimated in FY2026). The annual amortization charge will decline as shorter-lived intangibles fully amortize, but the core technology and customer relationship intangibles will persist for 7-11 more years. I estimate the GAAP/Non-GAAP gap will narrow from $1.52 to approximately $1.20-1.30 by FY2027 as shorter-lived intangibles roll off. The SBC component ($1.00/share) is likely to remain stable or increase slightly as AMD scales headcount.

## Response to Competitive Analyst

**Critique: Forensic analysis should assess whether AMD's R&D capitalization and expense recognition policies are comparable to NVIDIA's — differences could make margin comparisons misleading.**

Accept. This is a valid cross-company forensic concern. AMD expenses all R&D as incurred (standard for fabless semiconductors under US GAAP), and NVIDIA follows the same policy. However, differences in how the two companies allocate costs between COGS and R&D (e.g., tape-out costs, packaging R&D) could affect gross margin comparability. Without detailed line-item breakdowns from both companies' 10-Ks, I cannot confirm full comparability. I flag this as a [DATA GAP] that would require retrieving both companies' full 10-K filings and comparing cost allocation policies.

## Response to ESG & Governance Analyst

**Critique: $306M reserve release timing should be scrutinized against compensation measurement periods — if it inflated FY2025 non-GAAP operating income (50% weight in PRSU awards), the pay-for-performance linkage is weakened.**

Partially accept. The ESG Analyst raises a legitimate governance concern. The $306M release did boost FY2025 non-GAAP operating income by ~4-5%, which flows through to PRSU performance metrics. However, (1) the $800M original charge in Q2 2025 reduced FY2025 operating income by a larger amount, so the net FY2025 impact of China-related charges was -$494M, not +$306M; (2) the reserve release occurred in the same fiscal year as the original charge, meaning the net compensation effect is negative, not positive. The ESG Analyst's concern would be more valid if the charge and release spanned different fiscal years (charge in FY2024, release in FY2025), which they did not. I revise the assessment: the within-year charge/release dynamic slightly reduces but does not eliminate the governance concern.

## Response to Technical Analyst

**Critique: Post-earnings selloff (-17%) corroborates the forensic concern about margin quality — institutional investors saw through the 57% headline GM.**

Partially accept. The post-earnings selloff is consistent with the hypothesis that institutional investors discounted the reserve-release-inflated margin. However, post-earnings selloffs are multi-causal: China revenue uncertainty ($390M non-recurring), guidance quality ($9.8B vs. $9.4B consensus — above but not dramatically so), and broader market conditions also contributed. Attributing the entire selloff to the forensic margin concern overstates one factor among several. I maintain the margin quality finding as a contributing factor to the selloff but do not assign it as the primary cause.

**Critique: Data sourced from WebSearch rather than actual financial data reduces precision of forensic calculations.**

Accept. The forensic analysis relied on financial data from StockAnalysis.com (Tier 4) and AMD press releases (Tier 1) rather than full 10-K filings. While the headline figures (revenue, net income, CFO, balance sheet items) are reliable from these sources, detailed footnote analysis (off-balance-sheet items, revenue recognition policies, segment detail) requires the full 10-K text. The Beneish M-Score and Altman Z-Score calculations used the best available data, but precision could be improved with 10-K source data. I maintain the scores as directionally correct while flagging that a 10-K-based recalculation could shift individual component values.
