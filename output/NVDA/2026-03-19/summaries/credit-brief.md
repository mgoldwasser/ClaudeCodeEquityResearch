# Credit Brief — NVIDIA (NVDA)

**Analyst:** Credit Analyst

**Headline Finding:** NVIDIA's balance sheet is one of the strongest in corporate history with $51.6B net cash, 503x interest coverage, Debt/EBITDA of 0.08x, and Altman Z-Score of 6.64; credit risk is near-zero under any plausible business scenario; the single material credit risk is customer concentration (top 5 = 50% of revenue), which is a demand risk rather than a balance sheet risk.

**Fair Value Estimate:** [Balance sheet supports equity valuation unambiguously — no leverage constraints, no refinancing risk, $191B M&A capacity available at 1.0x Net Debt/EBITDA threshold]

**Biggest Risk/Concern:** Balance sheet is not a source of credit risk — it is a source of strategic optionality. The single credit-adjacent risk is that customer concentration (hyperscalers = 50% of revenue) could cause revenue collapse if capex spending reverses, which would pressure leverage ratios and potentially trigger covenant issues IF a revolving credit facility with financial covenants exists (not confirmed from available data). Even in a -70% revenue scenario, Total Debt/EBITDA reaches only 0.77x, well below distress levels. However, the "circular financing" commentary warrants monitoring: if AI companies overleveraged on GPU collateral contracts experience margin compression or defaults, NVIDIA's customer quality could be impaired even if balance sheet metrics remain pristine.

**Signal for Pass 2:**
- Credit analyst should verify whether NVIDIA has a backup revolving credit facility with financial covenants (common for commercial paper programs); if so, assess likely covenant thresholds and confirm headroom under base/bear scenarios.
- Confirm source of $15.6B goodwill jump in FY2026 from full 10-K Note on Acquisitions and Intangibles — if impairment required in a demand downturn, could create $3-10B earnings hit.
- Monitor off-balance-sheet supply chain commitments ($37-45B in purchase obligations); assess whether customer purchase order backing is confirmed for all material commitments.
- Watch for rating agency upgrade to AA/Aa1 in next 12-18 months if earnings sustainability demonstrated through a full AI capex cycle — ratings agencies have flagged positive outlook.
