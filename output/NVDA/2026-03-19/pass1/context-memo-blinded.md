## Company Summary
NVIDIA is a fabless semiconductor company that designs GPUs and accelerated computing platforms. In FY2026 (ended Jan 2026), Data Center accounted for 89.7% of revenue ($193.7B of $215.9B total), driven by AI training and inference demand. The company is the dominant supplier of AI accelerators with ~80% market share, defended by the CUDA software ecosystem (4M+ developers, 20-year head start).

## Why We're Looking Now
Three catalysts converge: (1) NVIDIA just reported record Q4 FY2026 results ($68.1B revenue, +73% YoY) and guided Q1 FY2027 to $78B, (2) GTC 2026 (March 16-19) saw Jensen Huang raise the revenue opportunity from $500B to $1 trillion and announce the Vera Rubin architecture, and (3) a cross-stock intelligence note from AMD research (2026-03-09) flagged credible competitive threats — AMD secured 12 GW GPU deals with OpenAI and Meta, and custom ASICs are growing 44.6% vs. GPUs at 16.1%. The question is whether NVIDIA's dominance is durable enough to justify continued premium positioning, or whether share erosion and demand normalization present material downside risk.

## Key Questions for Analysts
1. **Revenue trajectory:** Is $78B/quarter guidance sustainable through FY2027? What's the realistic FY2027 revenue range, and what growth deceleration curve applies?
2. **Margin durability:** Can 75% gross margins hold as competition intensifies from AMD MI450 and custom ASICs? What pricing power does NVIDIA retain?
3. **CUDA moat erosion:** Is OpenAI Triton + AMD ROCm improvement a 2-year or 10-year threat to CUDA lock-in?
4. **Demand durability:** Is AI infrastructure spending cyclical (capex cycle) or secular (essential infrastructure)? What does hyperscaler capex commitment through 2027 tell us?
5. **Custom silicon displacement:** Will custom ASICs (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia) structurally reduce NVIDIA's addressable market, or does the TAM grow fast enough to offset?

## Mandatory Demand & TAM Questions
- What are the 3-5 specific applications driving incremental demand over the next 5 years? For each, what is the current size, growth trajectory, and compute intensity?
- What new demand waves (applications that don't exist today or are pre-commercial) could materially expand the TAM? Model at least 3 scenarios.
- What is the historical CAGR for NVIDIA and the AI semiconductor sector? Is the projected growth rate consistent with early-stage, mid-stage, or late-stage technology adoption?
- Compare this sector's growth trajectory to at least 2 historical technology analogues (cloud, mobile, internet) at the same maturity stage.
- Does cheaper/better technology create MORE demand (Jevons paradox) or LESS demand (efficiency headwind)? Cite evidence.

## Mandatory Strategic Positioning Questions
