## 4. Outlier Flags

⚠️ **OUTLIER: INTC — P/E and Fwd P/E**
Intel's Fwd P/E of 89.8x is 2.45 standard deviations above the peer median of 26.1x. This is not a valuation premium but a denominator problem — Intel is expected to earn only $0.50 in FY2026 EPS vs. -$0.06 in FY2025, making fwd P/E mechanically elevated during a loss-to-profit transition. **Action:** Exclude INTC from P/E and Fwd P/E median calculations. INTC's EV/Revenue (4.4x LTM) is informative as a floor for a declining-revenue semiconductor business.

⚠️ **OUTLIER: AVGO — EV/Revenue (LTM 22.7x)**
Broadcom trades at 22.7x EV/LTM Revenue, which is 2.12 standard deviations above the peer median of 9.3x. However, AVGO's NTM revenue growth is 56%, the second-highest in the group (behind NVDA), and gross margins are 67.8%. The premium reflects the AI infrastructure buildout cycle and custom silicon opportunity. **Action:** Include in median; treat LTM EV/Revenue as a temporary growth-transition artifact — NTM EV/Revenue (15.6x) is more representative.

⚠️ **OUTLIER: MSFT — PEG (3.94x)**
Microsoft's PEG of 3.94x is 1.94 standard deviations above the peer median of 0.5x. MSFT's EPS growth is only ~24% NTM vs. its Fwd P/E of 23.2x — the PEG inflates because MSFT is a mature business commanding a platform premium. **Action:** MSFT PEG is not comparable to semiconductor-growth PEG ratios; flag as a structural misfit in the PEG analysis.

## 8. Key Findings Summary

NVDA sits at the extreme frontier of both quality and growth within this peer group. Every fundamental metric places it at the 100th percentile versus comps. The comps-implied valuation range using peer median multiples ($173–$229 weighted composite, $217 central Fwd P/E estimate) represents a structurally biased floor, not a ceiling — NVDA's 71% NTM revenue growth is 3-4x the peer median, and its 44.8% FCF margin exceeds that of every peer including Microsoft and Alphabet. Applying peer multiples mechanically to NVDA systematically undervalues the growth premium.

The most reliable valuation signals from this analysis are:

1. **Forward P/E at peer median (26.1x)** implies $217/share — this is the cleanest cross-peer comparison and the primary estimate.
2. **EV/FCF at peer median (49.8x)** implies $200/share — strong cross-check; FCF generation is real and recurring.
3. **Growth-adjusted (PEG 0.5x)** implies $288/share — this is the upper bound if the market chooses to fully reward growth.
4. **EV/Revenue (LTM 9.3x)** implies only $85/share — this is the structural floor if revenue growth stops entirely and the business is re-rated to peer median. Not a realistic bear case but shows the multiple compression risk.

**The single most important quantitative signal:** NVDA's consensus FY2027 Fwd P/E of ~21-22x is below the peer median Fwd P/E of 26.1x, despite NVDA growing earnings 69% vs. the peer average of ~25%. If consensus estimates are approximately correct, NVDA appears to trade at a *discount* to peers on a growth-adjusted basis. The market appears to be pricing in significant estimate risk (probability that $8.30 EPS is not achieved) rather than awarding the premium its growth profile would normally command.

### Primary conclusion: **Undervalued** relative to comps (growth-adjusted), **Fairly Valued** on pure multiple basis
### Confidence: **Medium** — large and heterogeneous comp set limits comparability. NVDA's profile is so differentiated from peers that mechanical comp analysis has limited utility. DCF analysis and industry demand analysis are more informative for this name.
