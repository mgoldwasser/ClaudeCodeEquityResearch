## 9. Catalyst Summary

**The single most important catalyst for NVIDIA over the next 12 months is the Vera Rubin production ramp combined with the August 2026 Q2 FY2027 earnings call** — effectively a two-part event: (1) does Rubin reach volume production on schedule in H2 2026, and (2) does the first Rubin revenue quarter in Q2 FY2027 confirm the $1T revenue trajectory Jensen Huang outlined at GTC? This is partially priced in — the market believes the base case, but the magnitude of upside (ahead-of-schedule ramp, 10x inference cost reduction pulling demand sharply forward) and the magnitude of downside (any delay reprices the entire FY2027 $1T narrative) create highly asymmetric outcomes that the options market has not fully captured.

The May 27, 2026 Q1 FY2027 earnings call is the nearest major inflection point and functions as the first real-time test of whether sequential growth toward $1T is intact. If Q2 FY2027 guidance clears $90B, the stock re-rates. If guidance disappoints or gross margins surprise negatively, the Rubin transition concern surfaces ahead of the physical data point.

**The catalyst calendar strongly supports the bull investment thesis through H1 2026** — the May earnings event, Rubin sampling confirmations, and sovereign AI deal flow are all near-term catalysts trending positive. **The thesis requires more patience and conviction through H2 2026**, when AMD MI450 benchmark data will provide the first real external test of NVIDIA's competitive position in the next hardware generation cycle. Investors who hold through May 27 will have substantially more information to assess the Rubin ramp and the AMD competitive response.
