## 1. Executive Summary

We rate AMD **HOLD** with a probability-weighted price target of **$192**, implying less than 1% upside from the current $190.40. The stock is approximately fairly valued after a 28% drawdown from its October 2025 high of $267, with the market correctly pricing the tension between AMD's unprecedented AI GPU opportunity (12 GW of committed mega-deal deployments with OpenAI and Meta, representing ~$200B in potential multi-year revenue) and the extreme execution risk required to capture it (MI450 ramp at scale never attempted, CUDA software gap of 30-99% effective performance, and custom ASIC cannibalization growing 44.6% vs. GPU shipments at 16.1%). H2 2026 is the most consequential period in AMD's history: MI450 production ramp, initial mega-deal deployments, and NVIDIA Vera Rubin's competitive response will collectively determine whether the stock re-rates toward $250+ or breaks below $140. The balance sheet is a genuine fortress (net cash $7.25B, A/A1 rated, 59x interest coverage), which means failure will be execution-driven, not solvency-driven. Conviction is capped by four unresolved contradictions: the fair value range spans $120-$250 across analyst methodologies, the bridge contract hypothesis (mega-deals as temporary GPU purchases before ASIC maturity) remains unfalsified, GAAP ROIC of 6.6% sits below any reasonable WACC estimate (suggesting current operations destroy value before mega-deal revenue materializes), and the team cannot agree whether the AI capex cycle has 6-18 months or 3-4 years of growth remaining. The single biggest risk is MI450 execution failure at scale — if the H2 2026 ramp stumbles, both mega-deals are jeopardized and the stock faces 35-50% downside.

At $130, AMD trades at ~20x FY2026E EPS with zero mega-deal premium — a rational bear floor supported by the non-AI business (EPYC + Client + Embedded generating ~$28B revenue with $6B+ FCF) and the $9.4B buyback authorization providing structural demand.

## 3. Business Overview

AMD is a $310B fabless semiconductor company generating $34.6B in FY2025 revenue across four segments: Data Center (48%, $16.6B — EPYC server CPUs + Instinct AI GPUs), Client (31%, $10.6B — Ryzen PC processors), Gaming (11%, $3.9B — console SoCs + Radeon GPUs), and Embedded (10%, $3.5B — Xilinx FPGAs). The company is 100% dependent on TSMC for leading-edge manufacturing.

The thesis hinges entirely on the Data Center segment, specifically the AI GPU sub-segment. AMD has signed two mega-deals — 6 GW each with OpenAI and Meta — representing ~$200B in potential multi-year revenue contingent on MI450 GPU deployment beginning H2 2026. These deals include 320M warrants at $0.01 exercise price (effectively free equity transfer to customers), creating an adverse dilution dynamic: maximum dilution occurs in the bull case where execution succeeds.

FY2025 was a record year: revenue grew 24.2% to $34.6B, Data Center revenue grew 94% to $16.6B, and non-GAAP EPS was $5.67. The stock subsequently declined 28% from its October high as the market digested warrant dilution implications and MI450 execution uncertainty. AMD refuses to disclose Instinct GPU revenue separately from Data Center — the primary thesis driver has no Tier 1 financial disclosure. ### Unresolved Risks

1. **Fair value range of $120-$250 across methodologies** — The DCF (post-rebuttal $175-190), comps ($184-190), Risk EV ($192), and DA-adjusted EV ($188-195) converge near $190, but the scenario ranges do not: bear cases span $84-$140 and bull cases span $223-$250. The team agrees on fair value but disagrees on the probability distribution around it.

2. **Bridge contract hypothesis** — The Devil's Advocate's argument that mega-deals are temporary GPU purchases while custom ASICs mature was not resolved. The warrant structure (5-6 year vesting, deployment milestones) provides counter-evidence of durable intent, but OpenAI's Broadcom ASIC deal being 67% larger than its AMD deal provides counter-evidence of bridge intent. 3. **ROIC vs. WACC** — GAAP ROIC of 6.6% is below any WACC estimate (13-16%), implying current operations destroy value. The Sector Analyst argues operational ROIC of 12-15% (excluding goodwill from Xilinx acquisition) is the relevant metric. The Quant Analyst added goodwill-adjusted ROIC of ~18%. No reconciliation achieved — the answer depends on whether Xilinx goodwill represents genuine franchise value or overpayment.

4. **AI capex cycle timing** — Macro Analyst argues 6-18 months of growth remaining based on historical capex cycle comparisons (telecom 1997-2001, cloud 2017-2019). Sector Analyst argues 3-4 years based on enterprise AI penetration at ~30% (Early Majority phase). Technical Analyst notes AMD peaked October 2025 while AI news continued improving — classic late-cycle price/fundamentals divergence. This disagreement fundamentally affects terminal value assumptions.

### Contrarian Thesis
**The Consensus View:** AMD is the #2 AI GPU company with massive mega-deal momentum, trading at a reasonable 28.6x forward P/E with 30%+ growth — a rare PEG under 1.0 in AI semiconductors.

**Why Consensus May Be Wrong:**
